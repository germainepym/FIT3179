Introduction

What is Times University Ranking System?

How is the results of World University Ranking calculated? Through Times University Ranking System, each university is judge through 5 core missions of world-class, global universities: Teaching, Research, Citation, International Outlook and Industry Income.

How the ranking is evaluated out of 100 percentage

Visualisation

Do we need a ranking system?

A ranking system is necessary because there are millions of students around the world that deserves to know which education institute is best for them. The map is normalized for each country's respective population: How many students are there per 10,000 population.

Iceland and Australia is shown to have the highest amount of students for every 10,000 people in their country while Indonesia and Nigeria has the least amount of students for every 10,000 people in their country.

Evaluating the Impact of Scores


The bubble plot above illustrates the ranking scores obtained by the top 10 universities in 2016. This shows the scores of each category that each university that has respectively obtained. The worst performing scores are both income and international, however since they weigh the least out of the 5 scores, it did not affect the overall scores heavily according to the bar chart below.


This bar chart contains the actual score of the Top 10 universities in 2016 after converting them to their respective percentage.
Remark: Hover over the bar chart to visualise the initial scores and converted scores.

Students should consider applying to these universities if they would like to make a contribution or breakthrough in their respective fields because of the high research, teaching and citation scores these universities have.


The box plot shows the summarised data of all 800 universities that was evaluated by Times University Ranking System. It is seen that most universities perform poorly on the research score with that category having the lowest median score of 22.1 out of the other 4 categories. This shows that most universities with the few exceptions of the top universities in the world fail to develope and publish substantial and well perceived by academic peers.


The scatter plot matrix shows the correlation between the 3 most important scores when in comes to evaluating a high ranking university. Research and teaching are both correlated. This can be explained as university uses the same resources, facilities, and personnel, producing teaching and research together. However, it seems that there is less of a correlation between research and citation and also between citation and teaching seen through this scatterplot matrix.
