Introduction

What is Times University Ranking System?

How is the results of World University Ranking calculated? Through Times University Ranking System, each university is judge through 5 core missions of world-class, global universities. The 5 categories are:
- Teaching
- Reasearch
- Citations
- Industry Income
- International Outlook

Teaching score is evaluated based on the learning environment and the quality of teaching of the university, while research score is based on their research productivity and whether how much high quality research the has university published and whether it is perceived well by academic peers around the world. Citation score is calculated based on whether it has research influence and whether it is highly cited by other scholars that are leaders in their respective fields. These 3 scores heavily influence the ranking of the university as they each carry 30 percentage weight out of 100.

Industry income is judge by the commercial impact of an institution’s research, which is itself a reflection of the industrial value of the research. Lastly, the international outlook score is evaluated by international to domestic student ratio, international to domestic staff ratio, and the proportion of research that involves international collaboration. Industry income carries 2.5 percentage weight out of 100 while international outlook score 7.5 percentage weight out of 100.
