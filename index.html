Introduction

What is Times University Ranking System?

How is the results of World University Ranking calculated? Through Times University Ranking System, each university is judge through 5 core missions of world-class, global universities. The 5 categories are Teaching, Research, Citation, International Outlook and Industry Income.

The image below illustrates how does the ranking is evaluated out of 100 percentage.

Visualisation

Do we need a ranking system?

A ranking system is necessary because there are millions of students around the world that deserves to know which education institute is best for them. The map below illustrates the amount of students in each country, normalised to their respective population. Iceland and Australia is shown to have the highest amount of students for every 10,000 people in their country while Indonesia and Nigeria has the least amount of students for every 10,000 people in their country.

Overview of University Rankings


The donut chart on the right illustrates the top 100 universities and shows which country is the better destination for education while the radial chart on the left shows the Top 10 Universities in the world in 2016. According to the data presented, United States of America is the country that contains the most high ranking universities.

Remark: California Institute of Technology (Caltech), Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich (ETH Zurich)


The bubble plot above illustrates the ranking scores obtained by the top 10 universities in 2016. This shows the scores of each category that each university that has respectively obtained. The worst performing scores are both income and international, however since they weigh the least out of the 5 scores, it did not affect the overall scores heavily. However, through this illustration we can see why these universities are high ranking.


This >bar chart contains the actual score of the Top 10 universities in 2016 after converting them to their respective percentage.

Through this chart, we can see that eventhough Caltech scored the lowest score for international, they were still ranked number 1 because they performed really well on teaching, citations and research scores.

Students should consider applying to these universities if they would like to make a huge contribution or breakthrough in their respective fields because of the high research, teaching and citation scores these universities have.


The box plot shows the summarised data of all 800 universities that was evaluated by Times University Ranking System. This plot shows that most universities perform poorly on the research score with that category having the lowest median score of 22.1 out of the other 4 categories. This shows that most universities with the few exceptions of the top universities in the world fail to develope and publish substantial and well perceived by academic peers.


The scatter plot matrix shows the correlation between the 3 most important scores when in comes to evaluating a high ranking university. According to the matrix, research and teaching are both correlated to each other because the plot looks like a straight line. Teaching and research are correlated to each other because the university uses the same resources, facilities, and personnel, producing teaching and research together.

While there is less of a correlation between research and citation and also between citation and teaching.
